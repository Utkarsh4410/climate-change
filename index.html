Understanding Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. While natural shifts occur, human activities have become the main driver of these changes, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels.

The Impact

The Greenhouse Effect

Burning fossil fuels generates greenhouse gas emissions that act like a blanket wrapped around the Earth, trapping the sun’s heat and raising temperatures.

Major Emitters

Examples of greenhouse gas emissions that are causing climate change include carbon dioxide and methane. Clearing land and forests can also release carbon dioxide. Landfills for garbage are a major source of methane emissions.

Human Activity

Energy, industry, transport, buildings, agriculture and land use are among the main emitters. Since the 1800s, our actions have profoundly reshaped the global climate system.
